# Illustrative Mechanisms You Could Study

<div style="height:1.5rem"></div>

Below are **illustrative examples of mechanisms** you might investigate.

These are **suggestions, not restrictions**.  
You may choose other systems if the mechanism is **clearly defined and structurally identifiable**.

## Application-Based Mechanisms

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Social Media Feeds** *(e.g. TikTok / Instagram)*

▪ **Ranking systems**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Content in your feed is **ranked before being shown**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Platforms prioritise posts using signals such as **engagement, recency, or predicted interest**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how ranking determines **what appears first** and what becomes visible.

▪ **Engagement feedback loops**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• User actions (likes, watch time, shares) reshape what the system shows next.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how interaction data **updates and reinforces future recommendations**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Streaming Platforms** *(e.g. Spotify / Netflix / YouTube)*

▪ **Similarity-based recommendation**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• The system suggests content **similar to what users previously consumed**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how similarity is computed using **behaviour patterns or learned embeddings**.

▪ **Exploration vs reinforcement**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Platforms must balance recommending **familiar content** and introducing **new items**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how systems prevent feeds from becoming **too repetitive or predictable**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Conversational AI** *(e.g. ChatGPT)*

▪ **Response selection**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Language models generate many possible continuations and must **select one to output**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how **probability ranking or sampling** influences the final response.

▪ **Reasoning vs standard generation**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Some systems introduce **reasoning steps or structured prompting** instead of direct completion.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how reasoning pipelines differ from **simple next-token generation**.

▪ **Context window limits**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Conversational systems remember **only a limited amount of prior text**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how limited context affects **long conversations and response quality**.

## Model Architecture Mechanisms

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Attention-based models (Transformers)**

▪ **Learning relationships across information**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Some models analyse input by allowing different parts of the data to **attend to one another**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how this structure helps models capture **long-range relationships in language or data**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Adversarial models (GANs)**

▪ **Learning through competition**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• One model **generates outputs**, while another **evaluates them**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how this competitive interaction improves the **realism of generated images or media**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Encoder–decoder models**

▪ **Compressing and reconstructing information**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• One component converts input into a **compact internal representation**, while another reconstructs the output.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how this structure enables tasks such as **translation, summarisation, or reconstruction**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **Diffusion-style models**

▪ **Gradual refinement**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Some generative systems create outputs through **many small refinement steps** rather than a single prediction.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how iterative refinement produces **detailed images or media**.

## Technique-Based Mechanisms

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **How systems represent information**

▪ **Encoding meaning into numbers**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• AI systems convert text, images, or behaviour into **numerical representations**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how vector representations enable **similarity search, clustering, or retrieval**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **How systems learn what to prioritise**

▪ **Training signals and objectives**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• AI models improve by adjusting themselves to **reduce error or maximise a reward signal**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how different objectives influence **what the system becomes good at**.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **How systems update themselves during learning**

▪ **Iterative improvement**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Models repeatedly adjust internal parameters based on **feedback from previous predictions**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how repeated updates gradually improve system performance.

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

■ **How systems generate outputs step-by-step**

▪ **Sequential generation**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Many AI systems produce results **one step at a time rather than all at once**.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Study how sequential generation influences **creativity, randomness, or consistency**.

## Key Advice

<div style="height:1.0rem"></div>

Do **not attempt to analyse everything**.

A strong submission focuses on **one clearly identifiable mechanism** and explains **how it shapes system behaviour**.
