The Prompt

Create an artifact using GenAI—visual, written, or hybrid.

Your submission must include four parts:

i. Annotation (500 words)

A written reflection that answers:

  • What did the AI want to give you? Describe the default, the average, the path of least resistance.

  • What did you make it give you instead? Where did you push, redirect, override?

  • What risks did you navigate? Identify at least two:

  • Legal (copyright, IP, liability)
  • Regulatory (institutional rules, platform terms)
  • Moral/ethical (harm, deception, manipulation)

  • Creative (convergence, mediocrity, losing your voice)

  • Where is the line? Point to specific moments in your artifact where AI’s contribution ends and yours begins.

ii. The Artifact

The artefact is the primary output of the case study assignment. It is a designed or written piece that demonstrates your engagement with AI. The format is flexible and can take the form of a document, research poster, website, video—whatever best suits your work. Please refer to the guidance specific to each case study for further details.

iii. Short-Form Video

A short-form video is a concise audiovisual presentation of no more than 90 seconds, designed to showcase and communicate the core argument and key insights of your case study. The focus is on presenting your work eBectively within limited viewing time, highlighting the central findings. This should not be confused with any other videos you might create as part of your ‘artefact.’ Video production techniques will be introduced during tutorials. Students may use standard, built-in tools available on their operating systems (e.g. Windows Clipchamp; macOS iMovie) or in presentation software (e.g. PowerPoint, Keynote). High production value is encouraged but is not required; clarity, coherence, and analytical integrity are prioritised.

iv. Specific Item: Evidence

Include at least one screenshot or transcript showing a moment of friction, resistance, or divergence in your process.

What We Grade

We are NOT grading:

  • Polish
  • Technical sophistication
  • “Correct” use of AI

We ARE grading:

Criterion What It Means
Deliberateness Did you make choices, or did you accept defaults?
Awareness Can you name the risks you navigated?
Divergence Is there something here that couldn’t have come from the machine alone?
Honesty Do you know where the AI ends and you begin?

Rubric

Criterion Excellent Adequate Insufficient
Deliberateness Clear, specific choices documented; knows why Some choices documented, vague on reasons Accepted defaults; no evidence of choice
Risk Awareness Names multiple risk types; nuanced navigation Names risks but superficially No risk awareness
Divergence Clear moments where AI was overridden; artifact couldn’t exist without human input Some divergence but mild Artifact is indistinguishable from raw AI output
Honesty Precise about where AI ends and human begins Somewhat clear Boundary is unclear or hidden
