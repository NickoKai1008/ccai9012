Based on the provided context, here is a summary of the location, main objectives, actions, and stakeholders of the energy plan document.

**Location:** The plan is for the community of **Aniak, Alaska**.

**Main Objectives:**
1.  To create an **Energy Action Plan** for tribal buildings in Aniak.
2.  To make the buildings **safer, more comfortable, and more energy efficient**.
3.  To provide a starting point and guide for future building retrofit projects.

**Key Actions:**
The project followed a multi-step procedure to complete the plan:
1.  **Building Selection & Goal Setting:** The Aniak Traditional Council selected seven tribal buildings and set goals for each.
2.  **Kick-off Meeting:** Project staff and the Council met to discuss goals, procedures, and resources.
3.  **Data Collection:** Gathering baseline information on building energy use, occupancy, and condition.
4.  **Energy Audits:** On-site assessments of each building were conducted to provide prioritized recommendations for improvements.
5.  **Drafting & Review:** A draft Energy Action Plan was created, presented to the Tribe for feedback, and then finalized.
6.  **Supplementary Planning:** The plan includes additional elements like a data monitoring plan, maintenance checklist, and information on funding and training opportunities.
7.  **Outreach:** Activities to publicize the project's goals and outcomes to the community and at conferences.

**Primary Stakeholders:**
*   **Aniak Traditional Council:** The tribal government that initiated the project, selected buildings, set goals, and provided feedback.
*   **Cold Climate Housing Research Center (CCHRC):** The lead organization facilitating the project, coordinating steps, and compiling the plan.
*   **Energy Audits of Alaska:** The contractor responsible for conducting the on-site energy assessments of the buildings.
*   **Building Staff & Maintenance Personnel:** Interviewed for baseline data and are the intended users of the maintenance and monitoring plans.
*   **Nuvista Electric Light & Power / CEMAI (Bertha Prince):** A regional energy partner contacted for coordination.
*   **U.S. Department of Energy Office of Indian Energy:** Provided grant funding for the project.
*   **Community of Aniak:** The ultimate beneficiaries, addressed through outreach materials and the goal of improved community buildings.

**Note:** The specific seven tribal buildings audited include the Tribal Office, VPSO Office, AVCP Office, a Duplex, the Community Center, and two farm buildings (small and large).
